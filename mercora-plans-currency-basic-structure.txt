Best subscription structure

Do not start with too many plans.

Use 3 plans max.

Recommended pricing logic

You need a structure that feels cheap enough for African SMEs to try, but not so cheap that OrderLink becomes a low-trust bargain tool.

My recommendation:

1. Starter

For small sellers testing online selling.

Include:

storefront
product listing limit
local merchant-direct payment
order tracking
receipt generation
basic branding
basic analytics

Price target:

₦5,000 to ₦8,000/month
or annual discount equivalent

This range is low enough to reduce resistance but high enough to avoid attracting only unserious users.

2. Growth

For merchants that want better conversion and managed checkout options.

Include:

everything in Starter
Flutterwave checkout
advanced order management
more products
custom branding improvements
better analytics
basic marketing tools

Price target:

₦12,000 to ₦20,000/month

This is likely your real money plan.

3. Pro / Business

For serious brands.

Include:

priority support
custom domain
staff accounts
advanced reporting
better automation
settlement visibility
premium storefront features
maybe lower platform transaction fee

Price target:

₦30,000 to ₦60,000/month

This should target merchants already making meaningful sales.

Best transaction fee structure

Do not rely only on subscription.
Do not rely only on transaction fees.

Use a hybrid model:

low monthly subscription
plus platform fee only on OrderLink-managed payments

That is the cleanest model.

Recommended platform fee

For OrderLink-managed payments:

start with 1.5% to 3% platform fee
on top of processor fees
or include processor fees in final pricing display where needed

For merchant-direct payments:

do not take transaction commission at launch unless you are adding real financial value there
instead make it part of subscription value

This matters because Flutterwave already charges collection fees that vary by country and method. Their help center currently lists examples such as Nigeria local cards at 2.0% total, Ghana local cards at 2.6%, Kenya local cards at 3.2%, South Africa local cards at 2.9% + ZAR1, and many international card routes at 4.8%. Transfer and payout fees also vary by country, with Nigeria bank payouts listed at NGN 10, NGN 25, or NGN 50 depending on payout size.

So if you add a heavy platform fee too early, merchants will feel punished.

My honest recommendation:

Starter plan: no extra OrderLink commission on merchant-direct local payments
Growth/Pro: 1.5% to 2.5% platform fee on OrderLink-managed payments
consider a lower fee for high-volume merchants
What will make merchants lose interest

These are the fastest merchant killers:

Too expensive too early

If you launch with something like:

₦15,000+ starter
plus 4% platform fee
plus processor fee
you will scare away small and mid-level merchants.
Confusing settlement

If a merchant cannot clearly answer:

how much customer paid
what Flutterwave took
what OrderLink took
what they are receiving
when they will receive it

they will distrust the platform.

Forced platform custody for all payments

Some merchants will prefer receiving local bank transfer directly. If you force everyone into your managed rails from day one, some will leave.

Currency confusion

If store prices jump around or settlement amounts look different from what they expected, trust breaks immediately.

Default currency: how to handle it properly

This is where many platforms get messy.

You need one merchant default currency, but you should separate it from what the buyer sees and what the processor charges.

The correct model

Each store should have:

1. Base currency
This is the merchant’s home/store currency.
Example: NGN.

Use it for:

merchant dashboard reporting
product default pricing
analytics
settlement preference

2. Display currency / presentment currency
This is what the buyer sees on the storefront.

Example:

buyer browsing from abroad may choose USD display
but the merchant’s base currency remains NGN

3. Charge currency
This is the actual currency used when payment is processed.

This depends on:

payment method
processor support
country support
your gateway routing rules

Flutterwave documents supported currencies and methods per region, such as NGN, GHS, KES, UGX, XAF, XOF, ZAR, GBP, EUR, and USD in certain supported collection contexts.

4. Settlement currency
This is what the merchant receives.

That may match base currency, or not, depending on payout setup and gateway support.

Best way to go about it at launch

Keep it strict and simple:

Launch rule

Each merchant selects one default store currency during onboarding.

Examples:

Nigerian merchant → NGN
Ghanaian merchant → GHS
Kenyan merchant → KES

That currency becomes:

default product pricing currency
dashboard currency
order accounting currency

Then add this logic:

Buyer-side currency display

You may allow the buyer to view prices in another currency, but only as an estimate unless you fully support charging in that currency.

So:

display converted estimate
clearly label it as approximate if needed
final checkout must show the actual charge currency before payment

That protects trust.

My strongest recommendation

At first:

allow one base currency per store
allow optional display conversion
only allow charge currencies that your payment backend can actually process cleanly
do not allow merchants to randomly create product prices in five currencies at launch

That would be a mistake.

Best onboarding setup for currency

During merchant onboarding, ask:

country
default business currency
payout currency
bank/payout country
whether they want direct local payments, OrderLink-managed payments, or both

Then backend rules should determine:

what payment methods to show
what charge currencies are allowed
whether international checkout can be enabled
whether settlement can happen automatically or manually

That is far better than leaving everything open-ended.

Settlement policy: best practice

Do not settle instantly at launch.

Use a settlement delay such as:

T+3 to T+7 business days for new merchants
shorter later for trusted merchants

Flutterwave states settlement timelines vary by payment method, and successful transactions first appear as collection balance before being processed to a linked bank account or wallet.

So your policy should be:

buyer pays
payment verified
merchant sees pending settlement
after hold window, merchant gets payout
dashboard shows fees and net amount clearly

This gives you room for:

failed payouts
refunds
suspicious transactions
disputes
Best method overall

If you want the best chance of success:

Do this
launch with merchant-direct + managed-payment hybrid
set one base currency per store
keep buyer currency switching mostly display-level at first
use 3 plans max
use hybrid revenue: subscription + managed-payment fee
keep your platform fee modest
make receipts automatic and trustworthy
make settlement logic transparent in dashboard
keep frontend extremely responsive, but let backend own financial truth
Do not do this
force all merchants into platform-managed payments immediately
support unlimited currencies per merchant at launch
let frontend calculate important financial totals
charge aggressive fees too early
build a visually polished platform with weak reconciliation underneath
My direct recommendation in one line

Launch with one store default currency, a hybrid payment model, modest subscription pricing, low platform fees on managed payments only, and a delayed transparent settlement system.

That is the cleanest, safest, and most commercially sensible path.
